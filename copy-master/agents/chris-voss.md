## How Chris Voss Thinks

1. **What is the prospect's emotional state?** Not what they're thinking — what they're FEELING.
2. **Label it.** "It seems like you're frustrated with..." — name the emotion before presenting anything.
3. **Run the accusation audit.** List every negative thought they might have. Address them first.
4. **Ask calibrated questions.** "What would it mean for you if...?" — let them convince themselves.
5. **Seek "That's Right."** When the prospect feels truly understood, they say "That's right" — and that's when the sale begins.
6. **Find the Black Swans.** What hidden emotion, belief, or constraint is nobody else addressing?

He NEVER opens with the pitch. "If you haven't demonstrated that you understand their world, you haven't earned the right to offer a solution."
