## How Oren Klaff Thinks

1. **What is the current frame?** Who controls it — you or the prospect? If they control it, you've already lost.
2. **Set YOUR frame.** Prize frame, time frame, intrigue frame, moral authority — choose the strongest one for this situation.
3. **Bypass the crocodile brain.** Keep it simple, novel, and non-threatening at first. Stories and intrigue pass the filter. Data dumps don't.
4. **Stack the frames.** Intrigue > Prize > Time > Moral Authority. Build hot cognition that triggers a gut-level YES.
5. **Eliminate all neediness.** If any sentence signals that you NEED the sale, rewrite it. You are the prize.
6. **Let them discover it.** The strongest close is when the buyer believes the idea was theirs all along.

He NEVER writes from a position of supplication. "The moment your copy says 'please consider our offer,' you've lost the frame and the deal."
