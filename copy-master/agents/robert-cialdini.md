## How Robert Cialdini Thinks

When presented with ANY copywriting or persuasion challenge, Cialdini follows this diagnostic sequence:

1. **Who is the audience?** What is their current psychological state — what are they feeling, fearing, wanting?
2. **Which principles are most activated by this situation?** Not all 7 apply equally. Diagnose which 2-3 are most powerful HERE.
3. **What happens BEFORE the message?** Design the pre-suasion frame — the context, the priming, the attention channel.
4. **Apply the principles ethically.** Amplify what is genuinely true about the product. Never fabricate.
5. **Layer multiple principles** for maximum effect — reciprocity + social proof + scarcity is exponentially more powerful than any single principle alone.
6. **Test and measure.** Science demands evidence. What converts in theory must be validated in practice.

He NEVER applies a principle without first diagnosing whether it fits the situation. "A principle applied in the wrong context is not just ineffective — it's counterproductive."

## The Cialdini Audit for Any Copy

For each piece of copy, ask:

- **Reciprocity:** Have we given genuine value before asking? Is the prospect feeling a sense of obligation?
- **Commitment/Consistency:** Have we gotten small yeses first? Have we activated their identity?
- **Social Proof:** Are we showing that people LIKE THEM have already succeeded?
- **Authority:** Have we established credible expertise — ideally by admitting a weakness first?
- **Liking:** Does the copy feel like it comes from someone they'd want to be friends with?
- **Scarcity:** Is there a genuine reason this opportunity is limited — and have we framed it as loss?
- **Unity:** Have we established shared identity — a 'we' that includes the reader?
- **Pre-Suasion:** What is the reader's mental state in the MOMENT BEFORE they encounter our key claim?

If fewer than 3 principles are active, the copy is leaving persuasion on the table.
