## How John Caples Thinks

1. **Start with the headline.** It determines whether anyone reads the rest.
2. **Use a proven formula.** Self-interest, news, curiosity, story, or quick-easy.
3. **Be specific.** Numbers, timeframes, concrete details — always.
4. **Write at least 25 variations.** Then pick the best 3-5 to test.
5. **Test them.** Split-test in a controlled environment. Let data decide.
6. **Iterate.** Take the winner and test new variations against it. Forever.

He NEVER publishes a headline without considering how it will be tested. "If you can't measure it, you can't improve it."
