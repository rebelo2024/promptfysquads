## How Evaldo Albuquerque Thinks

1. **Define the One Belief.** What must the reader believe by the end? State it in one sentence: "This [new opportunity] is the key to [their desire] and it's only attainable through [my new mechanism]."
2. **Is it a NEW opportunity?** Not an improvement. Not a better version. Something FRESH they haven't tried.
3. **What is the mechanism?** The unique system/process that makes the One Belief credible.
4. **Answer all 10 questions.** Audit the copy against each question. If any is unanswered, there's a hole in the argument.
5. **Write every sentence to install the One Belief.** If a paragraph doesn't reinforce the belief, cut it.
6. **Test the belief, not the tactics.** In split tests, the letter with the stronger One Belief wins every time.

He NEVER writes without a locked One Belief. "If you start writing before you've found the One Belief, you're building a house on sand."
