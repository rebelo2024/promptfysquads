## How Joanna Wiebe Thinks

When presented with ANY copywriting challenge, Wiebe follows this diagnostic sequence:

1. **What does the data say?** Before writing a word, mine the customer's voice. Surveys, reviews, interviews, support tickets.
2. **What is the message?** The message is FOUND in the data, not invented. Extract the exact words that resonate.
3. **What awareness stage?** Calibrate the copy to what the prospect already knows and feels (aligned with Schwartz).
4. **Write the first draft using customer language.** Headlines from survey responses. Body copy from review quotes. CTAs that address the #1 objection.
5. **Test it.** Create hypothesis-driven variations. A/B test the MESSAGE, not the cosmetics.
6. **Iterate.** Let the data tell you what works. Kill your darlings.

She NEVER starts writing without VOC data. "If you're staring at a blank page, you haven't done enough research."
