## 1. Headline & Hook

- [ ] Headline stops the reader — creates curiosity, urgency, or bold promise (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Lead paragraph hooks within the first two sentences (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Headline is specific, not vague or generic
- [ ] Sub-headlines guide the reader through the piece
- [ ] Opening addresses the reader's pain or desire directly
- [ ] Headline calibrated to correct awareness level (Schwartz scale)

## 2. Persuasion & Structure

- [ ] Every sentence compels the reader to read the next one (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Copy follows a logical persuasion framework (PAS, AIDA, RMBC, or equivalent)
- [ ] Benefits are emphasized over features
- [ ] Specific details and numbers used instead of vague claims
- [ ] Social proof, authority, or credibility elements present where appropriate
- [ ] Objections anticipated and addressed
- [ ] Unique mechanism named and explained

## 3. Offer & CTA

- [ ] The offer is crystal clear — reader knows exactly what they get (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Call to action is specific, urgent, and easy to follow (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Risk reversal present (guarantee, free trial, no-obligation language)
- [ ] Scarcity or urgency is logical and believable, not manufactured
- [ ] Value proposition is unmistakable
- [ ] Price anchoring strategy used (value > price clearly established)

## 4. Voice & Readability

- [ ] Written in the target audience's language and reading level
- [ ] Conversational tone — reads like one person talking to another
- [ ] No jargon unless the audience expects it
- [ ] Short paragraphs, varied sentence length, easy to scan
- [ ] Active voice dominant; passive voice used only intentionally

## 5. Technical Quality

- [ ] Grammar, spelling, and punctuation are correct
- [ ] No unsubstantiated or legally risky claims
- [ ] Formatting appropriate for the medium (email, LP, ad, etc.)
- [ ] Length appropriate for the context and platform

## 6. Conversion Readiness

- [ ] Would YOU buy/click/act based on this copy? (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Emotional triggers are authentic, not manipulative
- [ ] The piece has a single, clear objective — no competing CTAs
- [ ] Bullets are loaded with benefits, not flat descriptions

## 7. Persuasion Psychology (Cialdini + Warren)

- [ ] Minimum 3 of 7 Cialdini principles actively applied (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Cialdini principles woven naturally into copy, not bolted on (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Reciprocity: Free value or insight given before the ask
- [ ] Commitment/Consistency: Small agreements build toward the big yes
- [ ] Social Proof: Testimonials, numbers, or peer behavior referenced
- [ ] Authority: Credentials, expertise, or endorsements present
- [ ] Liking: Personal stories, relatability, shared struggles used
- [ ] Scarcity: Legitimate limitation or urgency (not manufactured)
- [ ] Unity: Shared identity or "people like us" language
- [ ] Minimum 2 of 5 Blair Warren levers activated
- [ ] Warren — Encourage dreams: Future-pacing the transformation
- [ ] Warren — Justify failures: "It's not your fault" or equivalent reframe
- [ ] Warren — Allay fears: Risk removal, safety messaging
- [ ] Warren — Confirm suspicions: Validating what they already believed
- [ ] Warren — Throw rocks at enemies: Common enemy or antagonist identified
- [ ] No Cialdini principle applied in a deceptive or manipulative manner
- [ ] Persuasion architecture documented (which principle, where, why)

## 8. Offer Architecture (Hormozi Value Equation)

*Apply only when the deliverable includes an offer. Mark [N/A] for pure content pieces.*

- [ ] Dream Outcome clearly defined and maximized
- [ ] Perceived Likelihood of Achievement supported with proof/guarantee
- [ ] Time Delay minimized with quick-start elements or fast-action bonuses
- [ ] Effort & Sacrifice minimized with done-for-you, templates, or shortcuts
- [ ] Value Equation scored: (Dream Outcome x Likelihood) / (Time x Effort) (CRITICAL when applicable)
- [ ] Bonus stack maps to specific Value Equation dimensions they improve
- [ ] Guarantee removes risk from buyer and transfers to seller
- [ ] Value-to-Price ratio is minimum 10:1 perceived
- [ ] Each bonus solves a related problem or accelerates the core result
- [ ] No bonus is unrelated to the core transformation

## PASS/FAIL Criteria

**PASS:** All CRITICAL items [x], fewer than 2 non-critical failures, AND Section 7 minimums met.
**REVISE:** All CRITICAL items [x] but 2+ non-critical failures OR Section 7 minimums not met.
**FAIL:** Any CRITICAL item unchecked.

**Scoring:**
- Sections 1-6: Standard quality gate (same as Copy Squad baseline)
- Section 7: Persuasion psychology gate (REQUIRED for Copy Master — differentiator from Copy Squad)
- Section 8: Offer architecture gate (REQUIRED when offer is present)
- A deliverable that passes Sections 1-6 but fails Section 7 receives REVISE verdict, not PASS
